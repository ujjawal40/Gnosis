# Variational Autoencoders (VAE)

## From Autoencoders to Generative Models

An autoencoder compresses and reconstructs. But it can't **generate** new data because the latent space has no structure — there's no guarantee that a random point in latent space decodes to something meaningful.

VAEs fix this by forcing the latent space to follow a known distribution (usually Gaussian).

---

## The Generative Story

A VAE assumes data is generated by:
1. Sample latent variable: z ~ N(0, I)
2. Generate data: x ~ p(x|z; θ)

**Training goal:** Learn θ (decoder) and an approximate posterior q(z|x; φ) (encoder).

---

## The Math: Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO)

We want to maximize log p(x) but can't compute it directly. Instead:

```
log p(x) ≥ E_q[log p(x|z)] - D_KL(q(z|x) || p(z))
           ──────────────    ─────────────────────────
           Reconstruction     KL Divergence
           (how well can z    (how close is the
            reconstruct x?)    encoder to N(0,I)?)
```

This lower bound (ELBO) is what we maximize.

### The Reparameterization Trick

We need to backprop through sampling z ~ q(z|x) = N(μ, σ²). But sampling is non-differentiable.

**Trick:** Instead of z ~ N(μ, σ²), write z = μ + σ · ε where ε ~ N(0, 1).

Now the randomness (ε) is external, and the gradient flows through μ and σ.

### KL Divergence (Closed Form)

For q(z|x) = N(μ, σ²) and p(z) = N(0, I):
```
D_KL = -0.5 Σ (1 + log(σ²) - μ² - σ²)
```

This has an analytical solution — no sampling needed.

---

## Why VAEs Matter

1. **Structured latent space:** Can interpolate between data points smoothly
2. **Principled generation:** Sample z ~ N(0,I), decode → new data
3. **Disentangled representations:** Different latent dims capture different factors
4. **Foundation for:** Stable Diffusion's VAE, VQ-VAE, hierarchical VAEs
